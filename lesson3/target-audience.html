Audience

The audience for a chamber of commerce website is typically business owners, entrepreneurs, and managers who are interested in networking, finding resources, and learning about business opportunities in the local community. The website may also appeal to community members who are interested in supporting local businesses and learning about local events and initiatives. This site also provides information about local attractions, events, and accommodations for tourists and visitors who are planning a trip to the area. Government officials at the local, state, and national levels may also visit the chamber of commerce website to learn about the business climate, economic development opportunities, and other important issues in the community.

Personas

  • Sarah is a 35-year-old business owner who runs a small clothing boutique in the downtown area of a mid-sized city. She is always looking for ways to network with other business owners and find resources to help her business grow. She frequently visits the chamber of commerce website to access the member directory, upcoming events calendar, and business resources such as funding opportunities and marketing tips. Sarah also enjoys reading articles and blog posts on the website about the local business community and economy
  • Tom is a 45-year-old tourist who is planning a family vacation to a small coastal town. He wants to learn more about the local attractions, restaurants, and accommodations before he arrives. He visits the chamber of commerce website to find information about local events, tours, and activities. Tom also uses the website to research hotels, vacation rentals, and other lodging options. He appreciates the website's interactive map and photo gallery, which allow him to get a better sense of the area before he arrives.

Scenarios

  • John just moved to a new town and is looking for local businesses to support. He visits the Chamber of Commerce website to browse their member directory and find out about upcoming events and promotions.
  • Sarah is the owner of a small business that recently opened in town. She wants to connect with other local business owners and find out about networking opportunities. She visits the Chamber of Commerce website to join their membership program and attend their monthly meetings.
  • Mike is a real estate agent who wants to attract more clients to the area. He visits the Chamber of Commerce website to learn about the town's demographics, economic trends, and major industries, so he can better market his services to potential homebuyers.
  • Lisa is planning a vacation and wants to explore the town's attractions and activities. She visits the Chamber of Commerce website to find out about local museums, parks, restaurants, and shopping districts, as well as any upcoming festivals or concerts.
  • Tom is the owner of a manufacturing company that is considering expanding to the area. He visits the Chamber of Commerce website to research the town's business climate, labor market, and transportation infrastructure, as well as to connect with local government officials and economic development agencies.
